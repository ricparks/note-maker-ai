#### Reviews
The Amadeus Quartet, joined here by violist Cecil Aronowitz, produced a definitive cycle of Mozart's string quintets for Deutsche Grammophon in the late 1960s. These recordings are widely celebrated for their Viennese warmth, technical polish, and deep understanding of Mozart's idiom. While Pitchfork does not typically review vintage classical releases, critics and classical guides like the Penguin Guide and AllMusic often cite the Amadeus/Aronowitz recordings as benchmark interpretations. The C Major quintet is praised for its expansive lyricism, while the C Minor is noted for its dramatic intensity, characteristic of Mozart's works in that key. The stereo engineering from DG is generally regarded as excellent for the era, offering a balanced and clear soundstage.
